Burger

A classic burger is the ultimate handheld meal—simple yet endlessly customizable. At its heart, it’s a seasoned beef patty, seared to juicy perfection, and nestled between two soft, toasted buns. The contrast between the crisp crust of the grilled meat and the pillowy texture of the bun is irresistible, and the flavor deepens with every topping—whether it’s melted cheese, tangy pickles, fresh lettuce, or a dollop of your favorite sauce.

Burgers can range from backyard basics to gourmet creations. Some opt for American cheese and ketchup; others elevate theirs with caramelized onions, fried eggs, or aioli. No matter how it’s built, the burger is a universally loved comfort food because of its satisfying bite, rich flavors, and the freedom to tailor every layer to personal taste. Whether grilled over flames or cooked on a stovetop, a good burger hits all the right notes.

Ingredients (Makes 4 burgers)

    For the patties:

  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef (80/20 for best juiciness)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • ½ tsp garlic powder (optional)
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce (optional)

    • For assembling:

  • 4 hamburger buns
  • 4 slices of cheese (e.g., American, cheddar, or Swiss)
  • Lettuce leaves
  • Tomato slices
  • Pickles
  • Sliced onions (raw or grilled)
  • Ketchup, mustard, or burger sauce
  • Butter or oil for toasting buns

Instructions

    Form the patties:

  1. In a bowl, gently mix ground beef with salt, pepper, and optional seasonings. Divide into 4 equal portions and shape into patties about ¾-inch thick. Make a slight indentation in the center of each patty with your thumb to help it cook evenly.

    2. Cook the patties:

  2. Heat a grill or skillet over medium-high heat. Cook the patties for 3–4 minutes per side for medium, flipping once. Add cheese during the last minute of cooking and cover to melt.

    3. Toast the buns:

  3. While the patties cook, lightly butter the insides of the buns and toast them in a pan or on the grill until golden brown.

    4. Assemble the burgers:

  4. Place each cooked patty on a bottom bun. Top with lettuce, tomato, pickles, onions, and your choice of sauce. Add the top bun, press lightly, and serve immediately.
